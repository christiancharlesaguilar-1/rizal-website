Literary Masterpieces

✦

Two revolutionary novels that awakened Filipino consciousness and sparked the fight for independence.

1887 • Berlin, Germany

Noli Me Tángere

(Touch Me Not)

Rizal's first novel exposes the corruption and abuse of the Spanish colonial government and clergy in the Philippines. Through the story of Crisóstomo Ibarra, it reveals the social cancer plaguing Filipino society.

Major Themes

  • Colonial oppression and abuse
  • Church corruption and hypocrisy
  • Social inequality and injustice
  • Filipino identity and nationalism
  • Education as liberation

Key Characters

Crisóstomo Ibarra

Protagonist, idealistic young Filipino

María Clara

Ibarra's beloved, symbol of Filipino women

Padre Dámaso

Corrupt Spanish friar, antagonist

Elías

Mysterious ally, revolutionary figure

Sisa

Tragic mother, symbol of suffering

❖

El Filibusterismo

(The Reign of Greed)

The sequel to Noli Me Tángere follows Simoun, the vengeful Ibarra who returns disguised as a wealthy jeweler to incite revolution. A darker tale that explores the limits of reform and the call for radical change.

Major Themes

  • Revolution vs. Reform
  • Revenge and redemption
  • Greed and moral corruption
  • Hope and despair
  • The cost of freedom

Key Characters

Simoun

Ibarra in disguise, revolutionary mastermind

Basilio

Medical student, Sisa's son

Isagani

Idealistic poet and student

Paulita Gómez

Isagani's love interest

Padre Florentino

Wise priest, voice of reason

1891 • Ghent, Belgium

Nationalism & Reforms

🇵🇭

Rizal's vision for Philippine independence through peaceful reform and national awakening.

La Liga Filipina

Founded July 3, 1892 in Manila

Aims

  • Unite the Filipino people into one vigorous and homogeneous body
  • Mutual protection in every want and necessity
  • Defense against all violence and injustice
  • Encouragement of education, agriculture, and commerce

Motto

"Unus Instar Omnium"

One Like All

The organization aimed to unite Filipinos regardless of social status, promoting equality and collective action for national progress through peaceful means.

Historical Note: La Liga Filipina was dissolved just days after its founding when Rizal was arrested and exiled to Dapitan. Despite its brief existence, it inspired the formation of the revolutionary Katipunan.

Propaganda Movement Reforms

Peaceful reforms advocated by Rizal and fellow ilustrados

1

Equality Before the Law

Equal rights and treatment for Filipinos and Spaniards under colonial law

2

Filipino Representation

Philippine representation in the Spanish Cortes (Parliament)

3

Freedom of Speech & Assembly

Right to free expression and peaceful assembly for all Filipinos

4

Secularization of Clergy

Filipino priests to hold positions in the Catholic Church

5

Educational Reform

Expansion of public education and establishment of schools

6

Abolition of Abuses

End to forced labor (polo) and tribute collection (bandala)

Rizal Monument, Luneta Park

Mi Último Adiós

(My Last Farewell)

"Farewell, dear Fatherland, clime of the sun caress'd,
Pearl of the Orient seas, our Eden lost!
Gladly now I go to give thee this faded life's best,
And were it brighter, fresher, or more blest,
Still would I give it thee, nor count the cost."

— Opening stanza

Written on the eve of his execution, this masterpiece became Rizal's final gift to the Filipino people. Hidden in an alcohol cooking stove, the poem was discovered by his family after his death.

Language

Spanish

Stanzas

14 Stanzas

The Execution

Location

Bagumbayan Field (now Luneta/Rizal Park)

The execution site is now a sacred ground, home to the Rizal Monument

Last Moments

Refused blindfold and knelt to face the firing squad

His final words: "Consummatum est" (It is finished)

At 7:03 AM on December 30, 1896, Dr. José Rizal faced the firing squad with remarkable composure. Shot in the back, he managed to turn his body as he fell, dying face up towards the morning sky. His execution sparked outrage and became the rallying cry for the Philippine Revolution.

Project Purpose

This website serves as a comprehensive digital resource dedicated to the life, works, and legacy of Dr. José Rizal, the Philippines' foremost national hero. Through this platform, we aim to:

📚 Educate

Provide accessible and engaging information about Rizal's contributions to Philippine independence and national identity.

🌟 Inspire

Encourage patriotism and civic consciousness among Filipinos, especially the youth, through Rizal's example.

🔍 Preserve

Keep alive the memory and teachings of Rizal for future generations through modern digital platforms.

💡 Apply

Demonstrate practical application of web development skills in creating meaningful educational content.
